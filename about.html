FLOT  ·  Forward Line of Troops

Where the thinking meets the fight.

FLOT is an advisory practice focused on one problem: senior military leaders are being asked to make consequential decisions inside decision cycles that AI is compressing beyond human reflex. The gap between what the algorithm recommends and what the commander understands is not a technical gap. It is a cognitive one — and it is where wars are lost.

The practice draws on direct experience advising commands across the Indo-Pacific theater, where the intersection of great-power competition, sensor saturation, and machine-generated courses of action has made the human dimension of command the central variable in operational outcomes. Algorithmic speed without cognitive integration is not an advantage. It is a liability with a very fast clock.

FLOT works with a small number of senior leaders and defense organizations — not at scale. Engagements are built around the specific terrain of a command: its decision architecture, its staff rhythms, its tolerance for machine-mediated risk. The work is written, briefed, and embedded depending on what the problem actually requires.

Christopher Ghorbani founded FLOT after a decade at the intersection of defense strategy, emerging technology, and organizational design. His writing on decision-making, AI integration, and the future of command has circulated among senior military and national security audiences across the Pacific. He is based in Hawaii.
